Broadband and Privacy in City Hall

*test* Until June of 2018, I worked in San Francisco at the Mayor’s Office of Civic Innovation.

I drafted the official privacy and net neutrality requirements for the citywide fiber network San Francisco is developing, which established and advanced a new framework adopted by other cities (e.g., pg 18 of Tacoma, WA procurement docs) and formally recognized by the FCC and the ACLU.

I also led the Blue Ribbon Panel on Municipal Fiber on behalf of the Mayor's Office, reports available here (write ups from participating organizations here and here).

Data Privacy Law

Here are some highlights from my work in data privacy law:

Staffed a major privacy class action lawsuit that the Northern District of California certified [PDF] in July of 2016.

Staffed an employee lawsuit in Los Angeles Superior Court for workplace surveillance.

Authored amicus briefs and regulatory comments to the Supreme Court and various federal agencies about police databases, the technical limits of "de-identification," and the risks of unchecked debt collection practices on social media.

Staffed a lawsuit before the D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals to challenge the airport body scanner program.

Served as a panelist at Federal Trade Commission workshops on victims of debt collector abuses (transcript here [PDF], start on page 12).

I am also a Certified Information Privacy Technologist (CIPT) by the International Association of Privacy Professionals.

Intellectual Property Work

I also have considerable experience in IP law:

Litigation and client service work in trademarks and copyrights, including first-chairing a mediation.

CLE presentations at the Internet Law Leadership Summit on web scraping law.
